This pie is perfect for a cold winter evening. I used beef cheek which is very succulent but needs to be cooked long and slow otherwise it can remain quite tough. After gently simmering in some onions, garlic, and other veggies, the filling was placed in a pre-cooked pastry base, topped, and oven cooked until crispy and golden.

**Method**:

Dice beef into cubed pieces, add salt and lightly toss in plain flour, fry beef until a nice golden crust is formed and remove from pan. Place thinly diced onions in the same oil the beef was seared in and cook slowly until onions become soft, add some butter, thyme, and balsamic vinegar and continue to gently cook. Once the onions become gooey, add the leeks, carrots, garlic, and mushrooms. Stir in beef stock and tomato chutney, add your ground cloves, bay leaves, and whole thyme sprigs along with extra water to cover all ingredients. Simmer low and slow for 2 to 3 hours or until beef begins to fall apart and liquid has reduced. Place pastry in tray and pre cook in oven for about 10 to 15 minutes or until golden. Add filling, cover with top pastry layer and brush with eggwash. Oven bake for roughly 30 to 35 minutes at 200C / 390F
